Small-group labs

Clarity Cohort: Mini classrooms without the chaos.

Cohort is for students who share the same class and want to work through it together—at a calmer pace, in a smaller room, with a coach who keeps everyone on track. Same teacher, same units, less noise.

What it is

Like class, but quieter and more intentional.

Clarity Cohort turns a handful of students from the same course into a steady mini-classroom. They bring their real notes, homework, and tests from school. We slow the material down, clear confusion, and give them time to practice—without trying to hold thirty other students at the same time.

Built around one real class.

Cohorts work best when everyone is in the same course (often with the same teacher). That way, examples, practice, and test prep all line up with what they are seeing in class.

  • Same syllabus and unit flow.
  • Shared upcoming quizzes, labs, and exams.
  • One place to ask all the "I didn't catch that" questions.

A mini team instead of a lone student.

Students see that they are not the only one confused, stuck, or behind. That alone can lower shame and make it easier to ask for help and try again.

  • Calm accountability: they show up because their friends do.
  • Space to compare approaches and see other ways of thinking.
  • Wins are shared, not isolated—confidence builds faster.
How it starts

From "my friend has a group" to a weekly lab.

A Cohort usually begins with one simple sentence: "My friend is in Clarity and I want to join their group." Parents coordinate, we check that the mix is right, and then that class turns into a weekly learning lab.

Step 1: Students talk.

A student hears that a classmate is getting support, or a small group realizes they are all confused by the same unit. They decide they'd rather work through it together than struggle alone.

  • The group is rooted in real friendships or shared classes.
  • Students start more relaxed because they already know who will be there.

Step 2: Parents & Clarity build the group.

One parent reaches out as the point person. We gather names, classes, and goals, make sure the mix is a good fit, and lock in a weekly time that works across families.

  • Same day and time each week for predictability.
  • Group size kept intentionally small (usually 3–5 students).
  • We confirm fit before we ever meet so no one feels constantly behind.
Environment

Everything helpful about class. None of the chaos.

A good classroom can be energizing—until it is too loud, too fast, or too full of distractions. Cohort keeps the benefits of learning with peers and removes the parts that overwhelm sensitive, thoughtful students.

What we keep from the classroom

  • Shared "aha" moments when a confusing unit finally clicks.
  • Hearing classmates ask the same questions they were afraid to say out loud.
  • Practicing with real test-style problems, not random worksheets.
  • Light accountability: they show up because others are waiting on them.

What we strip away on purpose

  • Background chatter, constant movement, and sensory overload.
  • Being rushed through content just to "cover" the chapter.
  • Being put on the spot in front of thirty peers.
  • The feeling that asking one more question will derail the whole class.
Weekly rhythm

What one Cohort week actually looks like.

Every lab runs on a predictable rhythm so students know what is coming: check in, clarify, practice, and plan. Same flow each time, adapted to what their teacher has on deck.

Beginning of session

  • Quick round of what is due, what units feel shaky, and what tests are coming.
  • We decide as a group what needs attention most so the time actually moves the needle.
  • Short reteach of the stickiest concept using examples that match how this group learns.

Middle & wrap-up

  • Guided practice on real problems—students talk through steps and compare approaches.
  • Quiet work time so concepts can settle while help is still in the room.
  • Clear next steps for the week: what to review, what to try differently, and what to bring back.
Fit matters

When Cohort shines—and when to start elsewhere.

Cohort sits between one-on-one support and full coaching. It is best when the group is aligned and the main barrier is pace, pressure, or overwhelm—not total confusion across the board.

Cohort is a strong fit if…

  • Students share the same course (or very close) and similar goals.
  • They want support but still prefer learning alongside a few peers.
  • They can handle a small, calm group without shutting down.
  • You want help staying on top of one specific class, not a full life overhaul.

Another tier might be better if…

  • Your student is significantly ahead or behind the rest of the group.
  • They completely freeze or shut down in front of any peers.
  • You need deep, ongoing support around systems, stress, and family rhythm (usually Elite).
  • Schedules change weekly and you cannot reasonably protect a group time.
Nuts & bolts

Structure, logistics, and what families can expect.

Cohort is designed to be straightforward to start and sustainable to keep. You bring a small group from the same class; we bring structure, clarity, and calm.

Structure at a glance

  • Group size: usually 3–5 students, capped to keep things focused.
  • Session length: ~60 minutes once per week.
  • Format: live online; local in-person options case-by-case.
  • Subjects: middle school through AP math and science.

Enrollment & investment

  • One parent reaches out as coordinator; we handle the rest of the onboarding.
  • Pricing typically sits between one-on-one and Elite, with the hourly rate shared across families.
  • Specific rates vary by season and schedule; we walk through current options in your Insight Session.
  • If we realize Cohort is not the right tier, we will say so and point you to a better fit.
